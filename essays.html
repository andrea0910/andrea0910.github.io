Quotes

Words I
carry

Sentences I've held onto. Some I return to when I'm stuck. Some just refuse to leave. Reflections where I have them.

“Fall seven times, stand up eight.”
— Japanese proverb

I applied to Stanford three times. Each rejection felt final. It wasn't. This proverb didn't make the waiting easier, but it made it legible — as if getting back up was simply the next move, not a heroic one.

“The most courageous act is still to think for yourself. Aloud.”
— Coco Chanel
“Not everything that is faced can be changed, but nothing can be changed until it is faced.”
— James Baldwin

This showed up for me first in the context of ethics — it's easy to look past inconvenient truths in data, in models, in people. You can't fix what you won't name.

“You don't lose anything by trying, but you miss the opportunity if you don't even try.”

Not a quote from one person — more of a lesson that kept surfacing throughout my time at Stanford, in different words across different conversations with family and friends. It never got old.

“Stop being patient and start asking yourself, how do I accomplish my 10-year plan in 6 months? You will probably fail but you will be a lot further ahead of the person who simply accepted it was going to take 10 years.”
— Elon Musk

This was the moment something clicked — when I was confused, thinking constantly but not knowing where to start, waiting for the right person or opportunity to find me. The quote reframed it: the waiting itself was the problem.

“Do something that's a bit more scary.”
— Jon Rubinstein

Growing means stepping out of my comfort zone — not just taking risks, but having the courage and confidence to do so, and staying open to change. Most people know this. The hard part is actually doing it. Maybe the natural first step is to truly understand my own strengths and weaknesses, fears and comforts, and goals. Because if I don't make even the smallest effort, I may be the one getting in my own way.

“None of us will succeed alone. Don't be shy to ask for help.”

There are more people willing to help than I remember. It's good to solve problems on my own, but I should also have the courage to ask — more people, more opinions, more perspectives — especially when I'm confused or unsure. A single no, or no response, doesn't mean everyone will say the same. And the more I practice asking, the better I get at it.

“You gotta suck before you're great. But the process of learning and becoming better is painful. Be okay with some of your deficiencies. Be patient, be kind, and stay in the game.”

This made me think about the importance of being vulnerable. Everyone has weaknesses — but even without knowing others', I often assume mine are more shameful, with an underlying fear that people will judge me. I'm not sure if it comes from not wanting to embarrass myself, or from comparing myself to others. But what's the point of hiding my authentic self, when everyone is where they are because they went through their own process — on their own time, at their own pace, on their own terms?

“Be honest with yourself and others. If you deal with reality, you're five steps ahead of most people.”

Dreaming big is important — but dreaming as if a miracle will happen isn't enough. I need to be honest with myself about reality, and build with ambition on top of that honesty. When I open my eyes to see things as they actually are, I can make better decisions, build stronger things, and move forward with more clarity.

“Being vulnerable means being courageous.”
